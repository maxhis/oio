## Design Context

### Users
Developers use OIO as a fast, repeat-visit navigation layer for tools, docs, and inspiration sites they actually reopen during real work. The job is to replace noisy bookmark folders with a browsable, dependable index that gets users to the right resource quickly.

### Brand Personality
Calm, crafted, developer-first. The interface should feel focused, trustworthy, and quietly polished rather than promotional or flashy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Warm editorial light theme with tactile panels, soft paper-like depth, and restrained green accents. Motion should feel subtle and useful, supporting orientation and feedback without turning the product into startup marketing theater.

### Design Principles
- Prioritize scanability, clarity, and low-friction reuse over visual spectacle.
- Use motion to explain state and hierarchy, not to call attention to itself.
- Keep surfaces tactile and calm with warm contrast, gentle layering, and disciplined accents.
- Respect reduced motion strictly and favor performant `transform` and `opacity` changes.
- Design for long-term utility and repeat visits, not novelty-driven interactions.
